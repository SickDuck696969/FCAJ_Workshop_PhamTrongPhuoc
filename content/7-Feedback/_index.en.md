After 12 weeks in the **First Cloud AI Journey (FCAJ)** program, I learned a lot from real practice, not just theory. Here is my personal reflection on the program.

#### 7.1 General Evaluation

##### Learning Environment

What I appreciated most was the learning atmosphere. I was encouraged to explore on my own, and when I got stuck, there was always someone willing to help me think through the problem.

##### Mentor Support

The mentors were very helpful because they showed me how to approach a problem, read logs, and find the root cause instead of just giving the answer.

##### Practicality of the Program

The most valuable part for me was the practicality. Using Amazon API Gateway, AWS Lambda, Amazon SQS, EventBridge, CloudWatch, Aurora, and AWS Backup made the Serverless architecture much easier to understand.

##### Skill Development Opportunities

The program also helped me improve small but important skills like reading docs, writing clearer notes, testing step by step, and using Git more consistently.

##### Work Ethic and Mutual Support

I also liked the sharing spirit in the community. When something broke, people usually tried to solve it together, which made it easier for me to ask questions.

##### Training Materials and Content

The materials were well structured, and the workshops gave me something concrete to build instead of just theory.

#### 7.2 Some Reflections

##### What I Liked Most About the Program

What I liked most was deploying things myself and seeing the result immediately. That helped me understand both design and debugging.

##### What I Hope the Program Improves

I would like more hands-on troubleshooting sessions, especially on logs, IAM issues, cost optimization, and deployment mistakes.

##### Would I Recommend the Program to Others?

Yes, I would recommend FCAJ to friends or students who want to get into Cloud Computing. It helped me build confidence and work in a more structured way.

#### 7.3 Suggestions and Wishes

##### Suggestions

I hope the program will include more mini competitions that allow teams to compete with one another, encouraging healthy competition and promoting a culture of learning and improvement.

##### Future Wishes

If I have the chance, I would like to join more advanced FCAJ programs in the future and keep contributing to the community.
